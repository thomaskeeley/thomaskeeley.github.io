GDP Findings

The United States GDP is well beyond most of the major countries in the world and shows a consistent upward trend from 2010-2016. China displays a significant climbing trend and nearing the United States. Relative to the GDP, the total government expenditures is consistent with the GDP as the United States spends twice as much as the second leading country.

Military Findings

Unsurprisingly, the military spending levels are partially consistent with the overall spending levels. However, Saudi Arabia is introduced into the analysis with high levels of military spending. When examining military spending relative to total government spending, Saudi Arabia is significantly higher with more than 25% of total expenditures going towards military spending. Though the United States has the highest level of total military spending, the U.S. only dedicates around 10% of total expenses. This is further reflected in examining military spending as a percentage of GDP. Nearly 10% of Saudi Arabia's GDP is attributed to military spending.

Education Findings

Education spending levels are examined as a percentage of total spending. Displayed the top 12 countries in education spending between 2010-2016. Brazil displays the highest level of education spending as a percentage of total government spending at slightly about 15%. This is over three times what is spent on military expenditures. The United States is third highest in relative education spending. The level of education spending is just slightly higher than that of military spending but both are consistent with each other at about 10%. India and Russia are the only two countries in this group to have higher levels of military spending than education, though India is only slightly higher. Russia, however, has a rate of military spending that is over double that of education spending. When examining the per person spending relative the the total GDP per person, the United States displays the highest rates of education spending with Germany closely behing. Relative to GDP, India's rate of education spending is significantly lower than many of the other major countries. Many of the top trending countries in education spending are smaller countries. Though there is a collection of trending education spending in the Central Asia region.

Healthcare Findings

Healthcare spending is examined as a percentage of the GDP in the 2010-2016 timerange. The United States is again at the top of spending rates when it comes to healthcare. Though there is a slightly different relationship to military spending with a rate of healthcare spending well over three times that of military spending. This rate is of education spending to military spending is considerably consistent with the other major countries except for Saudi Arabia. Saudi Arabia spending on military accounts for nearly double of the GDP compared to healthcare spending. The United States displays a very high ratio of healthcare spending to per person GDP rates relative to the other major countries and India is again at the bottom of this group with lower levels of per person spending on healthcare. The top trending countries in healthcare spending display less of a geographic pattern than that of education spending. Brazil, Sweden and Saudi Arabia are all major countries that are displaying an upward trend in healthcare spending with other geographically smaller countries such as Sierra Leone and Marshall Islands displaying the highest upward trending rates on healthcare spending.

Overall Findings

Country level data for military, education, and healthcare is inconistent throughout various sources. The variables for this type of data varies across particular data sources and it is difficult to analyze with 100% confidence as there are often missing values for certain variables and missing data altogether for particular countries. The analysis conducted for this project collated data from different sources and calculated at different levels to produce the graphics seen in this presentation. In order to analyze the data despite the missing values, the average values were calculated in the 2010-2016 range. This value was often used to display the country level trends for the spending categories. Much of the data preprocessing was done using excel. The first variable examined examined was military spending. For the purpose of this project moving forward, the top 10-15 countries from military spending were examined with regard to other spending variables. With these countries in mind, the values for the other spending variables were merged to produce country spending statistics across the various data sources. Much of bandwidth for this project was spent on data acquisition and preprocessing which is common for most data science related projects. The trends identified throughout the analysis are consistent with world thought and understanding of country level spending. The United States displays the highest levels of spending as the country is the leader in global GDP. However, the United States is also a little more balanced in terms of overall spending. Saudi Arabia displays a high level of military spending and does not even appear in the top 15 for education spending. Relative to other spending levels, Saudi Arabia spends significantly more on military than health and education. China is the only other country that is relatively close to the United States in GDP and is displaying a steady upward trend in expenditures and GDP. Many of the upward trending countries in terms of education and health spending are smaller countries that may average between 1-3% growth in 2010-2016. Overall, the spending levels for some of the largest countries are somewhat consistent with conventional thought, though there are some trends that set certain countries apart when it comes to distribution of spending.
